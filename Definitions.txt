Acoustic ray tracing -
is a geometrical modeling technique that simulates sound propagation by tracing paths of energy particles (rays) from a source to a receiver

impulse response -
a, typically digital, recording that captures how an acoustic enviroment react to a very short full frequency sound

Sabines formula - an empirical acoustic equation that calculates a rooms reverberation time (rT60)

specular reflection - Specular reflection is the mirror-like reflection of light (or other waves, in this case sound) from a smooth, polished surface, where rays reflect in a single direction.

diffuse reflection - the scattering of waves in many directions when striking a roughor uneven surface

snells law - an equation that dictateshow waves bend when crossing the boundry between media

Lamberts law - part of the beer lambert law is an equation to quantify the exponential loss of energy as a wave traveles through a uniformly absorbing medium

vector - a mathmatical or physical quantity that has both magnitude and direction

ISM - The Image-Source Method is a geometric acoustic algorithm used to simulate sound propagation and reflections in an enclosed space by creating virtual "image sources" reflected across wall planes

RIR - the same as Impulse Response, acts as a digital fingerprint

reflection order - defines the number of surfaces a sound wave hits before it reaches a receiver

scattering coeffecient - quantifies the ratio of non- specularly reflected acoustic energy to the total reflected energy from a surface, allowing us to simulate surface roughness
